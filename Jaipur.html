About Jaipur Hotels Things to Do

Jaipur is the capital and the largest city of the north-western Indian state of RajasthanJaipur is a popular tourist destination in India, forming a part of the west Golden triangle tourist circuit along with Delhi and Agra.

Cultural Beauty

Located in the eastern Rajasthan, this city of Jaipur is popular of the amazing forts and stupendous palaces. This royal town has magnificent architecture and is the first planned city of India. The buildings here are made with pink-painted sandstone which brings it the name of Pink City. With fantastic attractions all around the city, it invites tourists from all over the world. Jaipur's rich cultural heritage is displayed in the traditions, customs, lifestyle, art and architecture of this place.

History

Jaipur, named after Maharaja Jai Singh II, was founded with meticulous planning by Vidyadhar Bhattacharya. Jai Singh's lineage traces back to the Kushawaha Rajput clan, known for the majestic Amber Fort. Despite early challenges with Aurangzeb, Jai Singh's strategic alliances and governance expanded Jaipur's borders. Maharaja Ram Singh's pink city initiative symbolized hospitality. Post-independence, Jaipur merged into Rajasthan, with Man Singh II's reign marked by infrastructural development. Today, Jaipur stands as a blend of historical grandeur, architectural marvels, and a vibrant cultural heritage.

Major Tourist Attractions

Some of the major tourist attractions in Jaipur include Amber Palace, Elefantastic, Jantar Mantar, Hawa Mahal, Sheesh Mahal, City Palace of Jaipur, Elefanjoy Elephant Sanctuary, Birla Mandir Temple,Jaigarh Fort, Mukesh Art Gallery, etc.

Peak Time to Visit

November to March is the best time to visit Jaipur and other parts of Rajasthan as the weather ranges between 8-degree Celcius during nights to 32-degree Celcius in the day. Summers are extremely hot, and sightseeing isn't a very joyful experience.

Things to Do in Jaipur

  • Visit Amber Fort: Explore the grandeur of this historical fort, known for its intricate architecture and stunning views of the surrounding landscape.
  • City Palace: Discover the royal heritage at the City Palace complex, showcasing a blend of Rajput and Mughal architecture, museums, and courtyards.
  • Hawa Mahal: Admire the iconic "Palace of Winds" with its unique honeycomb structure, offering a glimpse into Jaipur's royal past.
  • Jantar Mantar: Marvel at the astronomical instruments at this UNESCO World Heritage Site, designed by Maharaja Jai Singh II.
  • Jaipur Markets: Shop for vibrant textiles, jewelry, handicrafts, and traditional Rajasthani artifacts at markets like Johari Bazaar and Bapu Bazaar.
  • Chokhi Dhani: Experience Rajasthani culture, food, folk performances, and activities at this ethnic village resort.
  • Jaipur Literature Festival: If visiting during January, attend one of the world's largest literary festivals held in Jaipur.
