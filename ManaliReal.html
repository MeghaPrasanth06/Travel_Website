Welcome to Manali

About Manali Hotels Things to Do

Manali is a town, near Kullu town in Kullu district in the Indian state of Himachal Pradesh. It is situated in the northern end of the Kullu Valley, formed by the Beas River.

Cultural Beauty

Arranged on the elevated hills of the Himalayas, Manali is a spot that has been untouched by commercialization and globalization. It is altogether unique and different in relation to rest of the Indian states and has its old world charm. The Culture of Manali is such that it portrays a sound synchronization between conservative qualities and current considerations. The cultural heritage of Manali is said to be very traditional and rural.

History

The history of Manali is quiet mythical. The history of this place is related to the reign of Mahabharata. It is believed that Manali was the home of lawgiver Lord Manu. Even the name Manali means house of Manu. There is a story behind Manali and is said that when Manu and other Gods were flooded, the seventh avatar of Lord Vishnu saved them by taking to dry lands and Manu found Manali as his refuge. Some say that during the 17th century, the idol of Lord Raghunathji was bought by Raja Jagat Singh to his thrones who till now a presiding deity and king of Manali.

Major Tourist Attractions

Some of the major tourist attractions in Manali include Kullu,Solang Valley, Jogini Waterfalls, Hadimba Temple, Gulaba, Van Vihar Park, Vashisht Temple, Rahala Falls, Manu Temple, Manikaran Sahib Gurudwara, etc.

Peak Time to Visit

The best time to visit Manali is during the summer months of March to June when the weather is pleasant with temperatures ranging between 10°C to 25°C. Whereas, if you're looking for snow activities and winter sports, the best time to visit Manali is during the winter months of October to February.

Things to Do in Manali

  • Visit Rohtang Pass: This high mountain pass offers breathtaking views of the surrounding Himalayan peaks and is a popular spot for adventure activities like skiing and snowboarding in winter.
  • Explore Solang Valley: Known for its scenic beauty and adventure sports, Solang Valley is great for activities like paragliding, zorbing, and quad biking.
  • Visit Hadimba Temple: This ancient wooden temple dedicated to Hidimba Devi, built in 1553, is surrounded by cedar forests and has a tranquil ambiance.
  • Take a trek to Beas Kund: For trekking enthusiasts, Beas Kund offers a moderately challenging trek with stunning views of the Beas River and snow-capped peaks.
  • Experience Old Manali: Wander through the narrow lanes of Old Manali to explore its quaint cafes, vibrant markets, and peaceful riverside spots, offering a more relaxed and authentic experience compared to the bustling main town.
